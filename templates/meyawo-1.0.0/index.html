Who Am I ?

About Me

I’m a remote sensing specialist with a Master’s in Water Resources, focused on satellite imagery analysis using Landsat, Sentinel, and MODIS data. My work bridges science, technology, and design—ranging from crop monitoring and water quality assessment to building smart agricultural systems. As a front-end developer specialized in Web-GIS, I create interactive geospatial platforms.
I’m the co-founder of Seefarm, a smart agriculture management system, and the creator of Pixartsat, a personal brand turning satellite imagery into artistic prints. I’ve worked on national-level projects in meteorology and smart farming, including dust storm early warning systems, crop mapping, water quality monitoring, and decision support systems for optimized irrigation. Passionate about nature, innovation, and visual storytelling, I’m always seeking ways to combine science, environment, and art to make meaningful impact.
